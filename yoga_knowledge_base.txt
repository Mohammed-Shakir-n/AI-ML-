TITLE: Benefits of Shavasana (Corpse Pose)
Shavasana calms the brain and helps relieve stress and mild depression. It relaxes the body, reduces headache, fatigue, and insomnia. It helps lower blood pressure.
SOURCE: Yoga Journal - Shavasana

TITLE: Headstand (Sirsasana) Contraindications
Avoid Headstand if you have back injury, headache, heart condition, high blood pressure, menstruation, or neck injury. Beginners should practice with a wall.
SOURCE: Yoga Basics - Inversions

TITLE: Pregnancy Yoga Guidelines
During the first trimester, avoid hot yoga. In the second and third trimesters, avoid lying on your back for long periods. Avoid deep twists and inversions. Focus on opening the hips.
SOURCE: Prenatal Yoga Guide

TITLE: Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana)
Strengthens the spine, stretches chest and lungs, shoulders, and abdomen. Firms the buttocks. Stimulates abdominal organs. Helps relieve stress and fatigue.
SOURCE: Asana Index

TITLE: Safety for Hernia Patients
If you have a hernia, avoid core-intensive poses like Boat Pose (Navasana) or heavy lifting poses. Avoid straining the abdominal wall. Consult a doctor before practice.
SOURCE: Medical Yoga Therapy

TITLE: Glaucoma and Inversions
People with glaucoma should avoid inversions like Headstand, Handstand, and Shoulderstand as they increase intraocular pressure. Downward dog should also be done with caution or avoided.
SOURCE: Eye Health & Yoga

TITLE: Warrior II (Virabhadrasana II)
Strengthens and stretches the legs and ankles. Stretches the groins, chest, and lungs, shoulders. Stimulates abdominal organs. Increases stamina.
SOURCE: Warrior Series Guide

TITLE: Yoga for High Blood Pressure
Avoid holding your breath (Kumbhaka) during pranayama. Avoid rapid transitions. Focus on cooling breaths like Sheetali.
SOURCE: HBP Yoga Management

TITLE: Paschimottanasana (Seated Forward Bend)
Calms the brain and helps relieve stress and mild depression. Stretches the spine, shoulders, and hamstrings. Stimulates the liver, kidneys, ovaries, and uterus.
SOURCE: Forward Bends 101

TITLE: Introduction to Pranayama
Pranayama is the practice of breath control. It connects the body and mind. Common techniques include Ujjayi (Victorious Breath) and Nadi Shodhana (Alternate Nostril Breathing).
SOURCE: Breathing Foundations
